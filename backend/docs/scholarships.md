# Study Abroad Scholarships & Funding

## US Government Scholarships

### Fulbright U.S. Student Program
- **Award Amount:** Full funding — tuition, living stipend, airfare, health insurance
- **Duration:** 9–12 months (academic year)
- **Eligibility:** US citizens; bachelor's degree required; no prior Fulbright award
- **Countries:** 160+ countries available
- **Application Deadline:** October 12 (campus deadline varies — typically early October)
- **Notification:** March–May of the following year
- **Competition:** ~4,000 grants awarded from ~11,000 applicants (~36% acceptance)
- **Requirements:** Language proficiency, affiliation with host country institution, compelling project proposal

### Gilman International Scholarship
- **Award Amount:** $5,000 average; up to $8,000 for Critical Need Languages
- **Duration:** Minimum 4 weeks study abroad program
- **Eligibility:** US citizens receiving Federal Pell Grant; enrolled in undergraduate degree
- **Deadline:** October (fall deadline for spring/summer programs); March (spring deadline for summer/fall programs)
- **Special Note:** Preference for students going to underrepresented destinations and non-traditional students
- **Website:** iie.org/gilman

### NSEP Boren Scholarships
- **Award Amount:** Up to $20,000 (undergraduate); up to $30,000 (graduate)
- **Duration:** 2 months to 2 years
- **Eligibility:** US citizens studying critical need languages in world regions underrepresented in study abroad
- **Priority Languages:** Arabic, Chinese (Mandarin), Hindi, Portuguese, Russian, Swahili
- **Deadline:** January 31 (undergraduate); January 28 (graduate)
- **Service Requirement:** Recipients must work in federal government for 1 year after graduation

## Private & Foundation Scholarships

### Rhodes Scholarship
- **Award Amount:** Full funding — tuition, living stipend, airfare for Oxford University
- **Duration:** 2 years (with possibility of 3rd year)
- **Eligibility:** US citizens ages 18–24 (at time of application); exceptional academic record
- **Deadline:** October (varies by state district)
- **Selection Criteria:** Academic excellence, leadership, character, commitment to service
- **Awards:** 32 American Rhodes Scholars selected per year

### Gates Cambridge Scholarship
- **Award Amount:** Full cost of study at Cambridge University (tuition + living allowance + airfare)
- **Award Amount Detail:** ~£17,500–£19,000/year maintenance allowance
- **Eligibility:** Non-UK citizens; any degree level; strong academic record
- **Deadline:** October 12 (US applicants); December 3 (all other countries)
- **Selection:** ~80 scholars chosen annually from thousands of applicants

### Chevening Scholarship (UK Government)
- **Award Amount:** Full funding for 1-year Master's degree in UK — tuition + living stipend + airfare
- **Stipend:** ~£1,200/month (London); ~£1,000/month (outside London)
- **Eligibility:** Citizens of eligible countries with 2+ years work experience; bachelor's degree
- **Deadline:** November 7
- **Commitment:** Return to home country for at least 2 years after completing scholarship

### DAAD Scholarship (Germany)
- **Award Amount:** €861/month (graduates); €1,200/month (doctoral students/postdocs)
- **Duration:** 10–24 months
- **Eligibility:** Students of all nationalities; studying or conducting research in Germany
- **Deadline:** Varies by program — typically October–November for following year
- **Language:** German language courses included for many programs

### Erasmus+ (Europe)
- **Award Amount:** €300–€800/month depending on destination country
- **Duration:** 3–12 months (study); 2–12 months (traineeship)
- **Eligibility:** Students enrolled at a participating European university; some programs open to non-EU students
- **Deadline:** Set by home university (typically February for fall semester)
- **Countries:** 33 European countries + partner countries worldwide

## University-Specific Scholarships

### Harvard International Study Program Grant
- **Amount:** Up to $5,000
- **Eligibility:** Harvard undergraduates on approved study abroad programs
- **Deadline:** February 1 (summer); October 1 (academic year)

### Stanford Bing Overseas Studies Grants
- **Amount:** Up to $10,000 for financial aid recipients
- **Eligibility:** Stanford students with demonstrated financial need on approved programs

### NYU Global Awards
- **Amount:** Partial to full scholarship
- **Eligibility:** NYU students at NYU global campuses (Abu Dhabi, Shanghai, Berlin, London, etc.)

## Emergency & Supplemental Funding

### Fund for Education Abroad (FEA)
- **Amount:** $1,500–$7,500
- **Eligibility:** US citizens/permanent residents who are underrepresented in study abroad
- **Focus:** First-generation college students, students with disabilities, LGBTQ+ students, STEM majors
- **Deadline:** February 1 (for summer/fall); October 1 (for spring)

### Critical Language Scholarship (CLS) — US Department of State
- **Award:** Fully funded 8-week intensive language program abroad
- **Languages:** Arabic, Azerbaijani, Bangla, Chinese, Hindi, Indonesian, Japanese, Korean, Persian, Portuguese, Punjabi, Russian, Swahili, Turkish, Urdu
- **Eligibility:** US citizens enrolled in US college/university
- **Deadline:** November (for following summer)

## Scholarship Application Tips

1. **Start early** — Most major scholarships have October–February deadlines for the following academic year
2. **Match your profile** — Target scholarships where you're a strong fit (nationality, major, destination)
3. **Letters of recommendation** — Request 3–4 months in advance from professors who know your work well
4. **Personal statement** — Address why THIS country, THIS program, and HOW it connects to your career goals
5. **Language test scores** — TOEFL/IELTS scores are required for most scholarships; take tests 6+ months early
6. **Apply to multiple scholarships** — Top students typically apply to 5–10 scholarships
7. **Check your university's study abroad office** — Many institutions have their own funding pools that go unapplied
