# Student Visa Requirements for Study Abroad

## United States — F-1 Student Visa

The F-1 visa is the most common student visa for full-time academic study in the US.

**Requirements:**
- Acceptance letter (Form I-20) from a SEVP-approved school
- Valid passport (must be valid at least 6 months beyond intended stay)
- DS-160 nonimmigrant visa application form
- SEVIS fee payment ($350 for F-1)
- Visa application fee ($160)
- Proof of financial support (bank statements showing ability to cover tuition + living costs)
- Academic transcripts and test scores (TOEFL/IELTS for non-native English speakers)

**Timeline:**
- Apply 3–4 months before program start date
- Embassy interview required; wait times vary by country (1–3 weeks typical)
- Visa valid for duration of status (D/S) — you can stay as long as you're enrolled

**Financial Requirement:**
- Must demonstrate funds for first year: typically $25,000–$65,000 depending on university
- Sponsor letter or bank statement required

## United States — J-1 Exchange Visitor Visa

The J-1 visa is for exchange programs, research scholars, and short-term academic training.

**Requirements:**
- DS-2019 form from the sponsoring organization
- SEVIS fee ($220 for J-1)
- Proof of English proficiency
- Financial documentation

**Key Difference from F-1:** J-1 has a 2-year home residency requirement for some categories.

## Europe — Schengen Student Visa (Type D)

For study programs longer than 90 days in Schengen Area countries (Germany, France, Netherlands, Spain, etc.).

**Requirements:**
- University acceptance letter
- Proof of accommodation (campus housing or rental agreement)
- Health insurance valid in the Schengen Area (minimum €30,000 coverage)
- Proof of financial means (€720/month in Germany; €615/month in France)
- Biometric photo
- Apostilled academic documents

**Timeline:**
- Apply at least 3 months before departure
- Processing time: 15–60 days depending on country
- Apply at the embassy/consulate of your primary destination country

**Country-Specific Notes:**
- **Germany:** Blocked account (Sperrkonto) required — deposit €11,208 (2024 amount)
- **France:** Campus France pre-enrollment required for many nationalities
- **Netherlands:** MVV (entry visa) needed before arrival for non-EU students

## United Kingdom — Student Visa (formerly Tier 4)

**Requirements:**
- Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from UK university
- English language test results (IELTS Academic 5.5–7.0 depending on level)
- Proof of funds: £1,334/month in London, £1,023/month outside London (for up to 9 months)
- Tuberculosis test result (for students from certain countries)
- Immigration Health Surcharge (£776/year for students)

**Deadline:** Apply up to 6 months before course start date. Decision typically within 3 weeks.

---

## Australia — Student Visa (Subclass 500)

**Requirements:**
- Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from Australian institution
- Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)
- Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement
- Financial evidence: AUD $21,041/year (student) + AUD $7,362 (accompanying spouse)

**Processing:** 4–6 weeks; apply online via ImmiAccount.

---

## Common Documents for All Student Visas

1. Valid passport (10-year passport recommended)
2. Passport-size photographs (biometric specifications)
3. University acceptance/enrollment letter
4. Academic transcripts (last 3 years)
5. Standardized test scores (SAT/ACT for US undergrad; GRE/GMAT for grad)
6. English proficiency scores (TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo)
7. Personal statement / Statement of Purpose
8. Financial documents (bank statements, scholarship letters, sponsor affidavits)
9. Proof of accommodation
10. Health insurance documentation
